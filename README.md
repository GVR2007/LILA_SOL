**New Alternative Approach: No Activated Carbon at All**

Here is a **completely different, even simpler approach** without any activated carbon (GAC). This removes the need to buy, store, or replace carbon bags every 1–2 years — perfect for villagers managing everything themselves.

### Core Idea of the New Approach
We rely more heavily on **natural biological processes** and **longer contact time** in the **Bio-Sand / Slow Sand Filter** (already the heart of the system). Research on biosand and slow sand filters in rural India and similar pond water shows they already reduce color, odor, and organics quite well through the biological layer (schmutzdecke) and sand bed — often making the water taste and smell acceptable without extra adsorption steps.

For your pond water (TDS ~737 mg/L, ammonia 1.13 mg/L, moderate organics likely causing mild odor/color), we enhance the bio-filter and add simple natural polishing instead of carbon.

### Revised System (Hybrid: Oorani Pond + Small Well)

**Flow:**
Oorani Pond (monsoon, large storage) **or** Small Well (dry 10 months) → Easy Hand/Treadle Pump (lift) → Coarse Mesh Screen → Sedimentation Tank → Enhanced Aeration → **Larger / Longer-Contact Bio-Sand Filter** → Low-dose Chlorination → Calcite/Dolomite Remineralization → Large Covered Storage Tank → Village Taps

**Key Changes (No Carbon):**
1. **Enhanced Bio-Sand / Slow Sand Filter** (main upgrade):
   - Make the filter **larger** or with **deeper sand layer** (60–80 cm instead of standard 50 cm) and **longer pause time** (water rests 12–24 hours between batches if possible).
   - This gives more time for the biological layer to break down organics, ammonia, and compounds causing smell/taste.
   - Studies show good reduction in color, odor, and organics with proper residence time — many rural users report the water tastes "normal" and acceptable.

2. **Better Aeration**:
   - Use a longer cascade or multiple small drops with stones/PVC pipes. More oxygen helps biological activity in the filter and naturally reduces some volatile organics causing odor.

3. **Natural Polishing Step** (instead of carbon):
   - Add a **simple layer of locally available materials** at the end of the bio-sand filter or in a small separate drum:
     - Crushed **coconut shell charcoal** (not activated — just burnt coconut shells from village kitchens or local mills — very cheap or free).
     - Or **rice husk ash** / **wood ash** (available locally).
     - Or a thin layer of **local clay** or **Moringa seed powder** (natural coagulant + mild adsorbent).
   - These are low-cost, locally sourced, and replacement is extremely simple (pour new material once a year).

4. **Pond & Well Management** (very important for odor control):
   - Annual desilting of Oorani to reduce decaying organics at the bottom (main source of bad smell).
   - Encourage simple floating plants or shade to reduce algae/organics in the pond.

**All Maintenance Tasks Remain Tiny (Only SHG Women):**

| Task                          | Frequency          | Task Description                          | Time     |
|-------------------------------|--------------------|-------------------------------------------|----------|
| Hand Pump                     | Daily              | Rotate among women                        | 10-15 min |
| Clean coarse mesh             | Weekly             | Rinse with water                          | 2 min    |
| Scrape top of Bio-Sand Filter | Every 1-3 months   | Scrape 1-2 cm sand with plate             | 15-20 min|
| Add Chlorine powder           | Weekly             | One small spoon                           | 5 min    |
| Add calcite stones            | Once a year        | Pour small amount                         | 10 min   |
| Replace natural polishing layer | Once a year      | Pour new rice husk ash / coconut charcoal | 10-15 min|
| Pond/Well desilting           | Once a year        | Community group work                      | 1 day    |

No heavy work. No buying special carbon from Madurai. Everything uses village or nearby local materials.

**Estimated Cost**: Still around ₹90,000 – ₹1,15,000 (slightly lower because no proper GAC drum needed; bigger bio-sand tank instead).

**Why This Works for Thanichiyam (2-month rain, 10-month dry):**
- Hybrid sources give reliability.
- Big storage tank bridges long dry periods.
- Enhanced bio-filter + better pond cleaning reduces organics naturally.
- Final water should have acceptable taste/odor for drinking (many similar rural pond systems in India work this way).
- If mild odor remains in peak summer, villagers can do final boiling for drinking water only (common practice).

This is **much simpler** for long-term village management — fewer things to buy or replace.

**Advantages over previous version**:
- Zero dependence on external activated carbon supply.
- Lower recurring cost.
- All materials ultra-local.
- Still very effective for ammonia (bio-filter strength) and basic organics.

If the water still has noticeable taste/odor after 1–2 months of operation, we can add a very small amount of local burnt coconut shell charcoal as polishing (cheaper and easier than commercial GAC).
